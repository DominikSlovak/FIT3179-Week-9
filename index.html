Take Care of Our Forests

Forests are some of the richest areas for biodiversity on the globe. They provide a variety of habitats for plants, animals, and micro-organisms. On average, a full-grown tree will absorb more than 48 pounds of carbon dioxide from the atmosphere in one year and release oxygen in exchange, which plays a part in regulating global warming.

Over the documented 30 years, there has been a loss of almost 17500 of forest land per 1000 hectares across the world.

Forest Disturbances

*The red line indicates the average.

Not only do forests deal with deforestation, wildfires make up a staggering 62% of natural forest disasters. Naturally, lightning is one of the biggest causes of forest fires. This is typically caused by lightning hiting dry vegetation. Volcanic activity has also proved to cause fires.

Man made wildfires also make up a significant portion of fires. Up to 85% of fires that have occured in the US have been due to carelessness. This includes acts such as smoking, using mosquito coil, candles, camp-fires, arson or a tribal ritual/tradition.

In terms of insects and diseases, insect pests and fungal diseases can damage shoots, leaves, crowns and stems, which can reduce tree growth and condition. If the damage is severe enough, it can cause trees to die.

Severe weather refers to high winds during storms, hurricanes, or tornadoes that can uproot trees, break branches, and cause extensive damage to forests. Heavy precipitation and flooding has been documented to erode forest soils.

Deforestation refers to the deliberate and permanent removal of forests or trees to clear land for various purposes.

Forestry usually refers to the practice of managing and harvesting forests for timber and other forest products in a sustainable manner.

Shifting agriculture, often known as slash-and-burn agriculture, is a traditional farming method in which land is cleared by cutting and burning vegetation before planting crops.

Urbanization represents the growth of cities and the expansion of urban areas, often resulting in increased infrastructure development and human habitation

Wildfires are uncontrolled fires that occur in forests, grasslands, or other vegetated areas, often due to natural causes or human activities.

Greenhouse Emissions

Greenhouse Gases are gases that trap heat in the atmosphere, and are the leading cause of climate change. This includes Carbon Dioxide, Methane, Nitrous Oxide and Fluorinated gases.

AFOLU takes second place for greenhouse gas emissions. This stands for Agriculture, Forestry, and Other Land Use. “Land use” describes the human purposes for which an area of land is managed, such as for agricultural, industrial, recreational, or residential activities. This includes forest land, cropland, grassland, wetlands, settlements, and other lands.

When forests are cleared, it leads to the release of greenhouse gases stored trees and vegetation back into the atmosphere.

What are we doing about Deforestation?

We are slowly implementing SFM.

SFM stands for Sustainable Forest Management. The definition given by Forest Europe is "The stewardship and use of forests and forest lands in a way, and at a rate, that maintains their biodiversity, productivity, regeneration capacity, vitality and their potential to fulfil, now and in the future, relevant ecological, economic and social functions, at local, national and global levels, and that does not cause damage to other ecosystems."
